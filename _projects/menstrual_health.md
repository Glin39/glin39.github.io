  <section class="context-section">
    <div class="row">
      <div class="col-md-8">
        <h3>Context & Background</h3>
        <p>Menstrual health extends beyond reproductive functions, influencing diet, exercise, sleep, and overall well-being through hormone fluctuations. Despite its critical role, menstrual health literacy remains limited, with existing period apps focusing narrowly on cycle monitoring while overlooking key health dimensions and contributing to delayed diagnoses.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="col-md-4">

            <li><strong>Methods:</strong> Deployment studies, workshops, prototyping</li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Process Overview -->
  <section class="process-section">
    <h3>Research Process</h3>
    <div class="process-timeline">
      <div class="process-step">
        <div class="step-number">01</div>
        <div class="step-content">
          <h4>Discovery Research</h4>
          <p>Deployment study with 50 participants over 90 days to understand how people use various technologies to understand their menstrual health.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="process-step">
        <div class="step-number">02</div>
        <div class="step-content">
          <h4>Design Workshop</h4>
          <p>Conducted workshops using mid-level prototypes to derive design requirements for tools that facilitate connections across complex multimodal health data.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="process-step">
        <div class="step-number">03</div>
        <div class="step-content">
          <h4>Prototype & Validation</h4>
          <p>Designed and deployed an app prototype with 20 participants over 100 days to study how users shift from narrow to holistic understandings of menstrual health.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>

  <!-- Study 1 -->
  <section class="study-section">
    <h3>Study 1: Understanding Current Practices</h3>
    <div class="row">
      <div class="col-md-6">
        <h4>Research Goal</h4>
        <p>Examine how access to less commonly known menstrual health signals through consumer health technologies can enhance users' awareness.</p>
        
        <h4>Key Findings</h4>
        <p>Users could expand their perception of menstrual cycles beyond menstruation, but struggled to relate data from diverse sources and understand interconnections between different health signals.</p>
      </div>

  <!-- Design Implications -->
  <section class="next-steps-section">
    <h3>Design Implications & Next Steps</h3>
    <table>
        <tr>
          <th>Design Goal</th>
          <th>Short-Term Actions</th>
          <th>Long-Term Vision</th>
        </tr>
      <tbody>
        <tr>
          <td>Expand users' perception of menstrual health beyond menstruation</td>
          <td>
            - Predict all menstrual phases from logs<br>
            - Add tutorials & tips explaining cycle and lifestyle links
          </td>
          <td>
            Menstrual health recognized beyond reproduction and integrated into education, public health, and social norms as a vital sign.
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Help users identify meaningful correlations in menstrual data</td>
          <td>
            - Log and correlate lifestyle/events with symptoms<br>
            - Overlay signals like HR or temp<br>
            - Create customizable dashboards
          </td>
          <td>
            Real-time trend detection powered by AI and professionals, with clear insights into user-specific patterns.
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Support goal-setting and decision-making</td>
          <td>
            - Guided prompts for reflection<br>
            - Example scenarios & recommendations<br>
            - Track goals and provide check-ins
          </td>
          <td>
            Tools evolve into proactive coaches offering cycle-aware, adaptive guidance and enhancing self-management.
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td>Reflect diverse cycle experiences & set realistic expectations</td>
          <td>
            - Show variability in data visualizations<br>
            - Debunk myths about "regular" cycles<br>
            - Promote healthy observation over alarm<br>
            - Enable filtered community discussions
          </td>
          <td>
            Empower users with realistic literacy on normal fluctuations vs red flags, leading to better health decisions.
          </td>
        </tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
